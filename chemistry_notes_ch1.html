Chemical Reactions And Equations

Chemical Reactions

The process in which two or more substances combine together to form product is called a chemical reactions.

Reactants: The substances which take part in a chemical reaction are called reactants.

Products: The substances which take part in a chemical reaction are called products.

Chemical Equations

The method of representing chemical reactions with the help of the formulae of the substances involved in it is known as chemical equations.

Ex- Zn + H2SO4 -----------> ZnSO4 + H2

Characteristics of a Chemical Reaction

(i) Evolution of gas.

Ex- Zn + H2SO4 ----------> ZnSO4 + H2

(ii) Formation of a precipitate (Precipitation reaction).

Ex- Pb(NO3)2 + KI ----------> PbI2 + KNO3

(iii) Change in color.

Ex- Fe + CuSO4 ----------> FeSO4 + Cu

(iv) Change in temperature.

Ex- Cao + H2O ----------> Ca(OH)2 + Heat

(v) Change in state.

Ex- H2(g) + O2(g) ----------> 2H2O(l)

Balanced And Unbalanced Chemical Equations

Balanced Chemical Equation

The Chemical Equations in which the number of atoms of different elements are equal in the reactants and products Balanced Chemical Equations.

Ex- H2(g) + O2(g) ----------> 2H2O(l)

Unbalanced Chemical Equations

The Chemical Equations in which the number of atoms of different elements are not equal in the reactants and products are called Unbalanced Chemical Equations.

Ex- H2(g) + O2(g) -----------> H2O(l)

Exothermic Reactions

The Chemical reactions in which heat is produced along with the formation of the products are called Exothermic reactions.

Ex- CaO + H2O -----------> Ca(OH)2 + Heat

Endothermic Reactions

The Chemical Reactions in which heat is absorbed are called Endothermic Reactions.

Ex- CaCO3 + Heat -----------> CaO + CO2

Types of Chemical Reactions

(i) Combination Reaction

The Chemical Reactions in which two or more sustances combine together to form a single product are called Combination Reactions.

Ex- CaO + H2O -----------> Ca(OH)2 + Heat

(ii) Decomposition Reaction

The Chemical Reactions in which a single substance breaks down into two or more simpler products are called Decomposition Reaction.

Types of Decomposition Reactions

Thermal Decomposition

The Chemical Reactions in which energy is used in the form of heat for the decomposition are called Thermal Decomposition.

Ex- CaCO3 + Heat -----------> CaO + CO2

Electrolytic Decomposition or Electrolysis

The Chemical Reaction which involve the use of electrical energy for the decomposition of the reactant molecules are called Electrolysis.

Ex- 2H2O (Electricity) -----------> H2 + O2

Photo Decomposition or Photolysis

The Chemical Reactions in which involve the use of light energy for the purpose of decomposition are called Photolysis.

Ex- 6CO2 + 12H2O (Sunlight,Chlorophyll) ------------> C6H12O6 + 6CO2 + 6H2O

(iii) Displacement Reaction

The Chemical Reactions in which the more reactive element displaces the less reactive element from it's compound is known as Displacement Reaction.

Ex- Zn + H2SO4 -----------> ZnSO4 + H2

(iv) Double-Displacement Reaction

The Chemical Reactions in which two different ions or group of atoms in the reactant molecules displaces each other are called Double-Displacement Reaction.

Ex- Na2SO4 + BaCl2 -----------> BaSO4 + 2NaCl

(v) Redox Reactions

The Chemical Reactions in which oxidation and reduction takes place simultaneously are called Redox Reactions.

Ex- MnO2 + HCl ----------> MnCl2 + H2O + Cl2

Effects of Oxidation

Corrosion

When a metal is attacked by the substances around it such as moisture, acids etc., it is said to be corrode and the process is called Corrosion.

Ex- Rusting of metal, Blackening of silver etc.

Rancidity

The process of oxidation of fats and oils present in the food items, result in change in taste and smell, is called rencidity.

Methods to prevent rancidity

  • Packing food items in air-tight containers.
  • By adding anti-oxidants to fat/oil containing food.
  • It can also be retarted by storing food items away from heat and light.
